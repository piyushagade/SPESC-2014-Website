  • Letter from The H.O.D's Desk


     
    The prime objective of the Department of Petroleum Engineering, since the commencement of a Bachelors program of Petroleum Engineering in 1983, has always been to nurture employable quality engineers. Along with the accredited program of BE Petroleum, the department also offers Bachelor of Engineering in Petrochemical Engineering, Master’s Program in Petroleum Engineering and Doctoral Program in Petroleum Engineering and also in Geology affiliated to Pune University.

    The truly interdisciplinary nature of this petroleum engineering curriculum demands presence of qualified, experienced and competent faculty members from diverse disciplines in science and engineering with an attitude to understand students at personal level and motivate them to strive for excellence along with conventional teaching. Most of the faculty members have appropriate professional experience which is critical in developing students beyond textbooks. The very aspect of personal relationship beyond classroom teaching by all available faculty members with the students has helped maintain an emotional bonding between them. The relationship continues even after completion of graduation and post-graduation as evident from frequent visits of alumni to department and their availability for mentoring of students. Constructive suggestions by the alumni are of great help in bridging the gap between industry and academia.

    The department has availability of necessary equipment and software support for the growth of students.  This has helped the faculty members to organize various students focussed activities as a part of ONGC Chair, Cairn Campus Connect, and SHELL MOU, Academic collaboration with Aker Solutions, MIT SPE Student Chapter, SPG Student Chapter and PETROTECH Student Chapter. Support given by Computer Modelling Group, CMG, Canada, Kappa Engineering, USA, The Petroleum Experts, USA, Schlumberger India, Midland Valley, USA, and Fekete, USA by providing academic license of their professional software has helped us improve quality of training of students in promoting petroleum engineering education.   

    The Gold Status MIT SPE Students Chapter, established in 1989, is one of the oldest, very active student chapters in India. This platform and the era of globalization have given students opportunities to participate in ATW as scribes, conferences to read papers and also to attend paper presentation contests at national and international level.  Since 2001, nine students have won prizes at international level and represented at highest level by the award won by Miss Neeti Joshi, a gold medallist of 2006-2007 batch, at the undergraduate students’ category at the Annual Technical Conference and Exhibition (ATCE) organized by SPE International at California, USA. This tradition has continued with the winning performance of Miss Rohini Poptani as a leader in IPTC education week in 2013, runners up performance of Debarprit Chandra at Las Vegas in SEG 2013 and participation as one of the four teams in in the EAGE competition at London by the group of Ankit Bhowmik, Debarprit Chandra, Himanshu Tyagi and other three members in June 2013.

    In 2006, SPE honoured Professor L K Kshirsagar with the prestigious Regional Service Award for Middle East Region in recognition of his contribution in petroleum engineering education and motivating students to achieve excellence. He is the only academician in India to receive this award.

    The MIT SPG Student Chapter has also received excellent support from the Society of Petroleum Geophysicists, SPG India who provided students opportunities to participate in different professional workshops organized as a part of Hyderabad 2012.

    Effective implementation of updated curriculum, intense co-curricular activities, and emphasis on value based education by management, excellent bonding of faculty members and students with continuous encouragement from management and industry have helped department to gain reputation in India and abroad.  As a result students are highly competitive, efficient with excellent soft skills and ready to serve the industry as per their requirement.

    Prof. P.B. Jadhav
    H.O.D. Petroleum Dept., MIT Pune
    Maharashtra Academy of Engineering &
    Educational Research (MAEER), Pune, India

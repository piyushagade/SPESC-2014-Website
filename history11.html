  • 2011

    The MIT-SPE Student Chapter (No. 5684) takes an active part in undertaking lot of student-centric activities throughout the year. The students from Second, Third and Final Year are encouraged to join SPE and enjoy the fruits of membership.

    Due to the persistence and enthusiasm of its members, the Student Chapter won the Gold Standard for 2011.

    Only 25 out of more than 200 student chapters throughout the world won this coveted award. The Gold Standard is an exceptional standard at which the student chapter performs in the categories of membership, planning, education, professionalism, service, support, and fundraising. As a result, the chapter has been formally invited to the 2011 Annual Technical Conference & Exhibition (ATCE).

    Additionally SPE offers a number of opportunities for the students to attend various conferences and seminars. Students from Third and Final Year have also attended various “Distinguished Lectures” organized by SPE Mumbai Section. Members of MIT-SPE Student chapter have served as scribes and student delegates at various Oil and Gas Conferences being hosted in India.

    The current membership of the MIT-SPE student chapter exceeds 150 which include students’ from F.E to B.E. and M.E as well. The chapter holds meetings regularly for organization of various events and guest-lectures conducted round the year.

    The SPE Students Chapter of MIT organized its Annual Industry Institute Interaction Program 2011 on the 18th, 19th and 20th March 2011. A symposium, “New and Innovative Technology in Upstream Oil Industry” was organized as a part of the month long series of technical competitions. The prize distribution function and panel discussion were held on the 19th evening for which Mr. P. K. Borthakur, Executive Director, Asset Manager, Bassein and Satellite, ONGC, Mumbai, was the Chief Guest.

    The Student Paper Presentation contest was held on Saturday, 19th March, 2011. The contest was based on the guidelines provided by SPE International and was judged by six eminent professionals who were drawn from oil and gas industry in India and outside.

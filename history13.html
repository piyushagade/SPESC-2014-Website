  • 2013

    Department  of  Petroleum  Engineering, Maharashtra  Institute  of Technology, and  the  Society of  Petroleum  Engineers, Student Chapter  of  MIT, organized  its  Annual Industry  Institute Interaction  Program–2013 (AIIIP)  for  the  academic  year  2013, on  the  1st  and  2nd  of  March  2013. A  symposium,  “New  and Innovative  Technology  in  Upstream  Oil  Industry”  was  organized  as a  part  of  a  series  of  technical  competitions  carried out  for  a  period  of  more  than  a  month. The  prize  distribution  function  and panel  discussion  were  held  in  the  evening  of  Saturday, 2nd  March.
    Mr. Atul Chandra, President, International  Business, Reliance Industries, Mumbai, were  the  honorable  Chief  Guest  for  this  function.

    The  symposium  was  divided  into
    a) Presentations  of  innovative  ideas  by students, highlights of various  competitions.
    b) Panel  Discussion  on  “Adapt  to  Advance”.
    c) Presentations  by  experts  from  Industry  as  a  part  of ONGC  Chair  Program

    Student Paper Presentation contest, on innovative idea, was held on Saturday, 2nd  March, 2013. Following students presented papers on a wide variety of topics listed below:

    The day started with the paper presentation competition. Dr. P. B. Jadhav, Head, Department of Petroleum Engineering, felicitated the Judges:
    > Dr.Salil Banerjee, Discipline Advisor, Reservoir Engineering, Shell Technology Center, Bangalore,
    > Mr.Sumit Bhat, Country Manager, Production Enhancement, Halliburton Offshore Services, Mumbai,
    > Mr. P.K. Kamble, Assistant Manager, Reservoir, GSPC,
    > Mr. A. K. Bhatia, GM, ONGC,

    > Mr. Amol Nakhle, Drilling Engineer, Schlumberger,
    > Mr. Amol Saxena, Sr. Petroleum Engineer, Reliance-BP, Mumbai.

    After the paper contest was completed the lunch break was utilized for the poster presentation and the photography competition.  After which the finalists of the different competitions Case study, Darcy Business Challenge, Model Making were held.

    Following were the various competitions and the first prize winners.

    The  MIT SPECK-13 magazine was released by all the panelists. Mr. Suboyin Abhijith the editor, spoke about the challenges in putting together the magazine which encompassed all the work done in the Petroleum Engineering Department throughout the year.

    Dr. Mangesh Karad, Executive Director, MIT, Dr. L. K. Kshirsagar, Principal and ONGC Chair Professor, MIT-Pune, and Dr. P. B. Jadhav felicitated the distinguished Guests of Honour.

    Mr. Bilal Ghansar,President, MIT-SPE Student Chapter, spoke about the chapter achievements and the different competitions which were held throughout the year.

    Dr. P. B. Jadhav, Head, Department of Petroleum Engineering, highlighted the achievements of the department and the help we are constantly receiving from SPE Mumbai and alumni. He spoke about the students who had been sent abroad for representing MIT.
    He considered this as a meeting of stake holders.

    Dr. L. K. Kshirsagar, Principal, MIT-Pune, acknowledged the help from the industry and thanked the Petroleum Industry Experts and alumni for attending the function and encouraging the young Petroleum Engineers. He spoke about the code of conduct recommended by SPE International and advised students to inculcate this code.

    A series of presentations by guests was organized on Sunday, 3rd  March, as a part of the ONGC Chair Program, covering nearly every aspect of the Petroleum Sector. The guest lectures were very interactive and captivating.

    Mr. Prakash Kamble, GSPC, an alumnus from MIT, concluded the guest lectures by encouraging the students and instilling in them the aspects of attitude and behavior.

    We gratefully acknowledge our corporate sponsors ONGC, Shell Technology India, Oil India, Cairn and individual sponsors, management of MIT-Pune for making the event successful.
